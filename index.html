Welcome

The Late Mrs. Lorna Delight Armstead, a Christian and a prominent Anglo-Indian established Harrow Hall in 1974 for the purpose of education of Anglo-Indians, Christians and all other Communities no matter which caste or creed.
Being a member of the Anglo-Indian Association, her aim was to bring about a civic sense, good clean habits and build a strong character so as to make them upright and disciplined citizens of India.

Our Difference

Our school infrastructure is made with renewed spaces in which students gets better environment, which results in greater learning potential. The students can learn in an environment that is both healthy and safe. Our school has smaller class size which assist the students to learn faster and perform better in smaller classes. They get more individual attention, increased participation, and better communication between the teacher and students.

Admission

Admission Fees/ Monthly Fees paid will not be refunded even if the student does not attend class or leaves the school. Fees have to be paid as long as the student’s name are in the rolls.
If a student is absent from class for 10 days or more without written information to the Principal/ Vice Principal, his name is liable to be struck off the rolls. She / he may be given READMISSION only at the discretion of the Principal after paying the necessary readmission Fees of Rs 3000.

Students who have been ABSENT for a day or more must write the reason for their absence in their Diaries which must be duly signed by Parents and counter signed by the Principal / Vice Principal before resuming class. In case of an illness exceeding 2 working days, a medical certificate must be given. Leave of absence exceeding 2 working days will not be granted without a previous written application from the parents. Absence on account of birthdays, marriage feasts and similar social occasions is discouraged as this affects the attendance and hampers the studies of the students.
One full month’s notice in writing must be given to the Principal by the parents or a month’s fees paid before a student may be withdrawn from the school. Transfer Certificate will be issued only after all dues are paid.
Cost of Transfer Certificate (All Classes): Rs 300/-
Cost of Outstation Transfer Certificate: Rs 400/-
The school reserves the right to increase the fees at any time of the year if an increment is considered necessary and every year 10 to 15% tuition fees will be raised to meet the yearly increment of the salaries and allowances.

Admission Procedure: Application must be made to the Principal and it is mandatory that parents accompany the candidates coming from another school, produce a Transfer Certificate, Last Report Card and evidence of age before joining classes in the new session.
All fees must be paid within 10th day of each month. If fees for the current month i.e., January is not paid by the 10th day of the month the student will not be permitted to attend class. In the event, if the Semester Examinations commence before the 10th day of the month, all fees must be cleared five days before the commencement of the Examination.
Class IX and X students are to pay Registration with the ICSE Board in the month of June 2019. Class X students are to pay their school fees for the months of January, February, March 2020 by December 10, 2019.

About Us

Harrow Hall is a co-educational institution open to all creeds and communities and to foreign nationals temporarily or permanently in India.
The School is under the management of The Societies Act. The School was established in 1974. The courses of study and the scheme of work are those leading to Indian Certificate Of Secondary Education Examination (I.C.S.E).
The courses of study and the scheme of work at school are those leading to the Indian Certificate Of Secondary Education(ICSE) Examination. The school is located at Karnani Mansions, 27B, Park Street, in the city of Kolkata and is open to children from all creeds and communities. The school is presided over by the Principal Mrs. J. Singh.

CO-CURRICULAR

The school is afflited to the Council of the Indian School Certificate Examnination, New Delhi.
The School Session is from April to March, Monday to Friday, Saturday and Sundays are the normal weekly holidays unless unforeseen circumstances compel the authorites to keep the school open. If this happen parents will be informed.
The medium of education is English. Bengali and Hindi are the vernacular language taught from classes KG to X.
Games and sports are an integral activity of the school's curriculum.

CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITES

1. Co-curricular activites are of paramount importance in the development of the child into a total integrated personality. Interpersonal relationship skils, confidence , poise and reliblity are some of the qualities developed through co-curricular activites. Hence, such activites are an essential part of the school programme.
2. The activites include Drama, Debate, Elocation, Art Competition, Talent Development, Quiz, Games, Physical Training, Social Work, NCC.
